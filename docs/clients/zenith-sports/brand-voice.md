## Voice rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

### 1. Concrete > Abstract
**❌ "Improve your skills with our innovative training solution."**
**✅ "10–15 min a day at home — your player switches back to a regulation ball and feels the difference instantly."**

The brand sells a tactile artifact. Every claim must be tangible.
Reference specific minutes, specific drills, specific physical
effects ("feels the difference instantly", "the coach noticed before
I did").

### 2. Methodology, not motivation
**❌ "Believe in your kid! Anything is possible!"**
**✅ "FIFA Size 3 control + FIFA Size 5 weight. Patent-pending. Built for technical players, not kick-and-run."**

TEKKY is the European technical-soccer methodology in a ball.
Speak the language of a methodology, not a motivational poster.
The customer is buying technical seriousness.

### 3. Coach-respect tone
Coaches buy methodology. Don't dumb it down. Don't over-explain.
Use real terms: *touch · first-time pass · close control · ball
mastery · ten-thousand-hour rule · technical training methodology.*
The reader is more soccer-literate than the writer assumes.

### 4. Founder voice (Philip first-person)
Most direct outreach is signed by Philip. He's casual but precise.
**Sample tone:**
> "Hi {{firstName}} — Philip @ TEKKY. Quick text to make sure you got
> my email about the training ball. Any questions before you order?
> Reply here, I'll personally get back to you. — P"

Notes:
- Signs with `— P` on SMS and short emails
- Uses "@ TEKKY" not "from TEKKY"
- "Quick" / "Quick note" / "Quick text" is a common opening qualifier
- Em-dash heavy in casual writing (matches Philip's actual style)
- Never "Dear" — always "Hi {{firstName}}"

### 5. Parent voice (testimonial-style framing)
Parent-targeted ads + emails lead with social proof in real-parent
voice. Real verbatims preferred over manufactured ones.
**Sample headline:** *"The coach noticed before I did."*
**Sample body:** *"A real parent on her son after 6 weeks with
TEKKY®. Smaller ball, FIFA Size 5 weight. Patent-pending."*

### 6. Player voice (skill-identity framing)
Player-targeted copy speaks to identity, not just outcomes:
*"You'll be the kid in your team who can do this."*
Avoid "improve your skills" — players don't think in those terms.
Players think in "moves I can pull off" and "stuff my friends can't do."

### 7. Forbidden words
Never use these in TEKKY copy:
- "Innovative" / "innovative solution" / "cutting-edge"
- "Synergy" / "leverage" / "ecosystem"
- "Game-changing" / "revolutionary"
- "World-class" / "best-in-class"
- "Take your game to the next level"
- "Don't miss out" / urgency-by-FOMO
- Generic motivational ("believe", "anything is possible", "limitless potential")
- "Solutions" as a noun

### 8. Concrete-claim policy (Rule 9 cascade)
Per AIOS principle 9 (content fidelity when porting from a real
source), TEKKY copy NEVER fabricates specifics. Patent-pending is
real. FIFA Size 3 control / Size 5 weight is real. Specific number
of minutes / weeks / drills must come from real product data or
real testimonial verbatims. **If a claim isn't verified, soften
or remove — never invent.**

## Drill-of-Week voice (coach-facing weekly broadcast)

Every Tuesday at 9am PT, a single drill goes to the coach list.
The voice for these is **half-coach, half-Philip's-personal-note**.
The drill itself is technical (cue, why, tier). Philip's intro is
casual + specific.

**Template structure:**
1. **Subject:** the drill name + a 4-6 word hook
2. **Opener (1-2 sentences in Philip voice):** what week it is, why
   THIS drill THIS week (e.g., "the one most U10s skip")
3. **The drill block:** name · cue · why · tier (Warm Up / Beginner /
   Intermediate)
4. **Embedded YouTube reference** (drill ID = video ID)
5. **Sign-off (1 line + `— P`):** invite reply or share

Forbidden in Drill-of-Week: motivational openers, generic
"hope you're well" niceties, asking for sales meetings.

## Voicemail script style (when audio clips ship)

Voicemails (currently `mediaUrl: null`, scriptHints only) follow
these rules when Philip records them:

- **Length:** 18-24 seconds. Hard cap at 28.
- **Open:** "Hey {{firstName}} — Philip from TEKKY." (not "this is
  Philip calling regarding...")
- **Body:** ONE specific reason for calling. Not three.
- **Close:** "Easiest way to reach me is just text this number back —
  I'll see it." (lowers callback friction)
- **Never:** quote a price · sound salesy · use "Account Executive"
  language · pretend it's the first contact when it's a follow-up

## Anti-patterns (real failures we've already debugged)

- ❌ Using "lead" or "prospect" in customer-facing copy → use
  "parent" / "coach" / "player" / "club" specifically
- ❌ Mixing audience voice (parent body in a coach email) — every
  audience funnel is segregated for a reason
- ❌ Calling the product "the ball" without TEKKY® context — first
  mention always TEKKY®, subsequent mentions can be "the ball"
- ❌ Adding emojis to coach-facing emails — coach voice is
  technical, not playful. Parent + player can use sparingly.
- ❌ Using "you guys" — gendered + casual in a way that reads off in
  written copy. Use "you" / "your team" / "your club"
