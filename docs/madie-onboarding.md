## 4 · How a call actually flows

1. Pick a lead from the Lead Picker → tick the checkbox → click
   **Start calling →**
2. The Call Workspace opens. You'll see the prospect's info (business
   name, phone, city, what we know about them), the **call script** in
   the middle, **objections + responses** on the right, and **outcome
   buttons** at the bottom.
3. Dial the number. Use the script as a guide — no need to read it
   word-for-word.
4. When the call ends (or rolls to voicemail), **click the matching
   outcome button**:
   - 🤝 **Booked meeting** (`answered_call_scheduled`) — the outcome
     we're aiming for
   - 📤 **Sent preview** / 📤 **Sent audit** / 📤 **Sent callback** —
     they engaged but didn't book on the call
   - 🙅 **Not interested** — they passed
   - 📞 **Voicemail** — you left one
   - 🤷 **No answer** / **Wrong number**
   - 🛑 **Take me off the list** — TCPA compliance; we never call
     again
5. Add notes if useful (optional — short context helps Ben later).
6. Click **Next prospect →** when you're ready.

**Every outcome you log shows up in the velocity strip within 60
seconds.** If you don't see it move, refresh the page once. If it's
still missing, just text Ben.

## 10 · When to ask for help

- **Right now (mid-call), warm transfer:** click **`Book Ben's call`**
  → pick the next slot. Ben confirms with the prospect.
- **Mid-call, can't transfer:** mark **`answered_callback`** and text
  Ben the prospect's name + what they wanted.
- **End of day, anything weird:** text Ben. Don't email — he checks
  texts faster.
- **Stuck on the dashboard:** the role badge in the top-right shows
  you're in `sales` mode. If it shows `owner`, log out and back in.

There's no such thing as a dumb question on Day 1 — ask early and
often.
