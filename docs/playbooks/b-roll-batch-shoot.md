# B-Roll Batch Shoot Playbook

> **Owner:** Ben · **Target time:** 2-4 hours · **Cadence:** monthly (next: 2026-06-18)
>
> **Why:** Per Hormozi content volume math (35K pieces/yr = 100x typical $1M biz), the content engine is the single largest free-CAC source over a 6-month window. The bottleneck is NEVER "what should I post" — it's "I don't have anything to post." A 2-4 hour batch shoot produces ammo for 30 days of daily posting. Reference: `aios/.claude/skills/content_engine/` + `reference_hormozi_content_brand.md` (SPCL framework).
>
> **Output target:** 30+ raw clips (each 5-60 sec) that get cut up into 60-100 finished pieces across 4 platforms.

## Pre-shoot checklist (do tonight before sleeping — 10 min)

- [ ] Phone fully charged + battery pack
- [ ] Tripod / phone mount accessible (or windowsill / coffee mug stack)
- [ ] 2-3 outfit changes hung up (creates visual "series" clusters)
- [ ] Whiteboard / notepad with 5 marker colors at the desk
- [ ] Clean desk surface (1 laptop, 1 mug, 1 notebook — that's it; clutter kills the frame)
- [ ] Lighting: shoot between 9 AM and 3 PM Sequim time, face a window (NOT a backlit one)
- [ ] No music in background during recording
- [ ] iPhone Settings → Camera → Record Video → 4K at 30fps · Stereo OFF

## Day-of: 30-second mental warm-up

Read this in the morning before recording:

> "Today is shooting day, not editing day. Bad takes are ammo for later. Don't critique. Don't redo. Just run the list. Future-me will pick the best 30 clips."

## The shot list — 30 raw clips, ~2-4 hours total

### Section A — Hooks (~30 min, 6 vertical clips)

These are the "stop the scroll" first 1-3 seconds of every Reel/Short/TikTok. Record ALL six even if you only use two.

| # | Hook line (spoken, look at camera) | Setting |
|---|---|---|
| A1 | "If your website has these 3 things, you're losing $1,000 a month and don't know it." | Desk, laptop in frame |
| A2 | "I built 4 websites this week. Here's the one that scared me the most." | Walking past a window, casual |
| A3 | "Your distributor isn't your partner. They're your competitor. Here's the proof." | Kitchen, coffee in hand |
| A4 | "Most websites are leaky buckets. Here's the hole that's costing you the most." | Whiteboard, drawing a bucket-with-hole |
| A5 | "I scraped 1,700 small-business websites this month. 95% had the same mistake." | Desk, screen of code/dashboard visible behind |
| A6 | "Stop running ads to a website that doesn't capture leads. Watch this." | Phone-held selfie, outdoors |

### Section B — Talking-head explainers (~60 min, 8 clips)

Each 30-60 seconds. Looking at camera. Conversational, not scripted-perfect. ONE camera angle the whole time so editing is cuts not zooms.

| # | Topic | Beats |
|---|---|---|
| B1 | "The 3 leaks every product manufacturer has on their website" | (1) no email capture (2) distributor owns the customer (3) no series funnel for repeat buyers |
| B2 | "Why I charge $997 for a website but $10K for the system" | The website is a brochure; the system is a machine; here's the difference |
| B3 | "The Hormozi rule that changed how I price BlueJays" | Charge until people say no, not what feels comfortable; tell the story of moving from $497 → $997 → $10K |
| B4 | "I built an AI that answers my customers in my voice" | Show the Madie/AI responder; explain it never sleeps |
| B5 | "Why every business owner needs an llms.txt file in 2026" | AI crawlers parsing your site for ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity answers; if you're not in their index, you don't exist |
| B6 | "What I learned scraping every small-business website in Sequim" | Specific stat (e.g. "82% don't have a phone number above the fold") + the fix |
| B7 | "Your Google Business Profile is doing more than your website is" | Most leads come from Maps not website; here's how to win both |
| B8 | "The single Squarespace mistake that's killing your conversion" | Decision-fatigue from too many template choices; the right answer is "pick whatever, then test" |

Pure visual ammo for cutaways. Each clip 5-15 seconds. Lock the camera; do the action; cut.

| C3 | Top-down shot of laptop + notebook + pen |

### Section D — "Day in the life" running shots (~30 min, 4 clips)

Quick montages for "I built this" type pieces. Each clip 10-20 sec.

| # | Shot |
|---|---|
| D1 | Walking from front door to desk with coffee, sitting down, opening laptop |
| D2 | Quick desk tour: pointing at 2-3 things (monitor / book / sticky note) |
| D3 | Window view of Sequim (golden hour if possible) + cut back to laptop |
| D4 | Texting on phone (visible "lead alert" notification + a thumbs-up reply) |

### Section E — Whiteboard mini-lessons (~30 min, 4 clips)

Same whiteboard, draw + explain. 30-60 sec each. These crush on LinkedIn.

| # | Topic | What to draw |
|---|---|---|
| E1 | The Audit Funnel | URL → Audit Page → Score → Reveal → Call → Close (with $ at each stage) |
| E2 | The Hormozi Value Equation | Dream Outcome × Likelihood ÷ Time × Effort |
| E3 | Why $997 is the right price | 3 columns: $97 (commodity), $997 (premium feel, sustainable margin), $10K (system / DFY) |
| E4 | The 4-Step BlueJays System | Find → Build → Ship → Optimize (with arrows + a flywheel feel) |

## Post-shoot (do same day, ~30 min)

1. AirDrop / move all clips into one folder: `04 Marketing Assets/Content/Raw/2026-05-19-batch/`
2. Scrub through ONCE. Flag the keepers (~30 of the 32) with a star emoji rename: `⭐A1-hook-leaky-buckets.mp4`
3. Drop the keepers into the editing pipeline (CapCut / Descript / whatever lives in `aios/.claude/skills/video_editor/`)
4. Pick 3 to publish THIS WEEK (one for Reels, one for LinkedIn, one for Shorts) — don't sit on them

## Posting cadence target

| Platform | Format | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram Reels | 9:16, 15-60 sec | 1/day weekdays |
| LinkedIn | 16:9 or 1:1, 60-180 sec | 1/day weekdays, longer copy |
| YouTube Shorts | 9:16, 30-60 sec | 3/week |
| TikTok | 9:16, 15-30 sec | daily, low-friction posting |

30 raw clips × 4 platforms × 2-3 cuts per clip = ~240 finished pieces. That's a month of daily posting from one shoot.

## What NOT to do today

- Don't edit while shooting. Edit tomorrow.
- Don't re-shoot a take just because you stumbled on a word. Imperfect = native to the feed.
- Don't add B-roll narration in your head — just shoot the shot. Voiceover happens in edit.
- Don't pick "the perfect outfit" — you have 3, rotate them, move on.
- Don't film talking-head outside in direct sun. Window light only.
