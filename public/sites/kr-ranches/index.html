Our Story

A Family Ranch in the Heart of the Yakima Valley

KR Ranches has been raising livestock on the sunny, fertile land around Prosser, Washington for over fifteen years. We started with a simple belief: families deserve to know where their food comes from — and the farmer who raised it.

We don't use feedlots. We don't use growth hormones. We don't rush the process. Our animals live on open pasture, eat what they were designed to eat, and are processed locally so the chain of trust stays short.

No Hormones, Ever

We've never used artificial growth hormones. What you eat is exactly what we raised.

Know Your Farmer

You can call us, visit us, and ask us anything. That transparency is the whole point.

Local & Loyal

We're a Prosser family operation. Your dollars stay in this community.

How It Works

Ordering direct from the ranch is easier than you think. No grocery store middlemen — just you and the farmer.

01

Reach Out

Call, text, or email us with what you're looking for. We'll talk through animal availability, sizing, and custom cut preferences.

02

Reserve Your Animal

We take a deposit to reserve your whole or half animal. Space is limited each season — getting on the list early is how you don't miss out.

03

Custom Processing

We work with a USDA-inspected local processor. You choose your cuts — steaks, roasts, ground, sausage — and they package it to your spec.

04

Pick Up or Delivery

Collect your order from the ranch in Prosser, or ask about seasonal delivery options for the Yakima Valley and Tri-Cities area.

Why Farm-Direct

The Difference Is Real

Grocery store meat travels thousands of miles and sits in centralized facilities. Ours doesn't. Here's what that means for you.

Full Transparency

You know the ranch, the farmer, the pasture, and the process. No black box. No mystery ingredients.

Better Nutrition

Grass-fed, pasture-raised meat has higher omega-3 content, more vitamins, and better fat profiles than conventionally raised alternatives.

Fresher Longer

Vacuum-sealed and flash-frozen at the processor, your order stays freezer-fresh for 12–18 months — far longer than store-packaged meat.

Better Value

Buying in bulk direct from the farm is more economical per pound than premium butcher shop prices — and the quality beats both.

Supports Local

Your purchase keeps a Prosser family operation alive and reinforces the local agricultural heritage of the Yakima Valley.

Ethically Raised

Our animals live low-stress lives on open pasture. Humane practices aren't just the right thing — they produce better meat.

Frequently Asked

Everything first-time buyers want to know before ordering from the ranch.

A half beef is approximately 200–250 lbs of packaged meat and requires roughly 8–10 cubic feet of freezer space. A standard 7-cubic-foot chest freezer works for most half orders. A whole beef (400–500 lbs) needs 16–20 cubic feet.

Yes, we take a deposit to hold your spot on the processing schedule. The deposit amount varies by animal type and size — contact us for current pricing and availability. The remainder is due at pickup.

Absolutely. We provide a custom cut sheet that you fill out before processing. You specify steak thickness, roast sizes, burger blend, whether you want bones, organs, fat for rendering — every detail is up to you. First-time buyers, we walk you through it.

Most orders are picked up at our ranch or the processor in the Prosser area. We occasionally offer seasonal delivery runs to Yakima, Richland, Kennewick, and Pasco. Reach out to ask about current delivery options when you place your order.

Pricing is typically quoted per pound of hanging weight or finished weight, depending on the animal. Processing and cut-and-wrap fees are separate and paid directly to the processor. Contact us for current prices — we're always transparent about what you're paying for and why.

Our beef cattle are primarily Angus and Angus-cross, known for marbling and tenderness. Our pigs are heritage breeds, and our lambs are well-suited to the Yakima Valley's grasslands. We're always happy to share more about our herd — just ask.
