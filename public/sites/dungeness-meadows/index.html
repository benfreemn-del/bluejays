About the Community

A neighborhood the river runs through.

Dungeness Meadows sits on a sun-drenched bend of the Dungeness River, tucked between the foothills of the Olympic Mountains and the calm waters of the Strait of Juan de Fuca. It's a private homeowners' association — about 200 residences — built around a shared sense of place and a quiet, well-kept rhythm of life.

What started decades ago as a small set of homes among the meadows has grown into a true community: a clubhouse where neighbors gather, a golf course shaped by the land, walking trails that follow the river, and a board of volunteer homeowners who keep it all running.

"You feel the mountains behind you and the water out ahead. Sequim sun on your back, and the river always close."

We're proud to be one of Sequim's most established residential communities — and to keep it that way, year after year, through good stewardship, simple rules, and neighbors who look out for each other.

The Setting

Where the rain stops and the sun stays.

The Olympic Mountains form a wall to our south and west — clouds rolling in from the Pacific drop their rain on the far side, leaving us with one of the driest, sunniest pockets on the entire West Coast. Locals call it the Sequim rain shadow. You'll call it home.

What's Inside the Gate

Eight amenities that make this a community, not just a subdivision.

Every fee you pay is reinvested into the place you live — the course, the clubhouse, the trails, the water that comes out of your tap. This is what your dues pay for.

Member-owned · Maintained year-round
01

Golf Course

A community course shaped by the meadow itself — open to members, easy to walk, and beautiful in every season.

02

Swimming Pool

A heated community pool open seasonally for residents and their guests — short walk from any door in the neighborhood.

03

Clubhouse

The heart of the community. Reserve it for birthdays, anniversaries, board meetings, or just a place to host neighbors.

04

Hiking Trails

Walk the river, climb the foothills, or follow the meadow loops — quiet trails maintained for residents year-round.

A dedicated, member-only storage area for RVs, boats, and trailers. Keep your driveway clear and your toys secure.

06

We own and operate our own water system — clean, reliable, and run by the people who drink from it.

An on-site security presence that knows the community and looks out for it — neighbors first, professional support second.

A members-only yard-waste drop point. No bag fees, no trips into town — just keep the meadows looking like meadows.

Governance

Run by the homeowners who live here.

No outside management company, no anonymous overseer — just volunteer neighbors, the bylaws they keep, and the committees that make sure things run.

Volunteer-led · Member-elected
I

Board of Directors

Elected by the membership. The board sets the budget, maintains the assets, and answers to the homeowners directly.

  • Monthly working sessions
  • Public-facing minutes
  • Open to member input year-round
II

Architectural Control

The ACC reviews exterior changes — new construction, paint, roofing, fences — to keep the look of the community consistent and protect property values.

  • Submit changes via a request form
  • Typical review within two weeks
  • Plain-English guidelines, not red tape
III

Committees

Working groups of volunteer homeowners handle the day-to-day: water, roads, social events, golf, security. There's always a seat at the table.

  • Water & Infrastructure
  • Recreation & Social
  • Communications & Welcome

Beyond the Gate

The whole Olympic Peninsula is your backyard.

Sequim sits at a crossroads — mountains behind you, water in front, cities a short drive or a ferry ride away. Here's what's close.

2 miles

Downtown Sequim

Groceries, dining, medical services, hardware, the farmers' market — all within minutes.

15 minutes

Strait of Juan de Fuca

Dungeness Spit, the longest natural sand spit in the country, plus tidepools, beaches, and lighthouse views.

1 hour

Olympic National Park

Hurricane Ridge, ancient rainforest, alpine lakes — one of the most biologically diverse parks in America.

2.5 hours

Seattle

Ferries from Bainbridge or Edmonds put downtown Seattle within an easy day trip — concerts, sports, the airport.

Ferry

Victoria, BC

The Coho ferry from Port Angeles crosses the strait to Victoria — gardens, harbor walks, high tea.

Annual

Sequim Lavender Festival

Mid-July. Sequim is the lavender capital of North America — the whole region turns purple.
