Why Radon Testing Matters on the Olympic Peninsula

Radon kills more Americans every year than house fires, drowning, and home invasions combined. Most people who die from radon-induced lung cancer never knew their home was elevated.

🗺️

The Peninsula sits in EPA Zone 2

Most of Clallam, Jefferson, and Kitsap counties are EPA Zone 2 (moderate radon potential, average 2-4 pCi/L), with localized Zone 1 hotspots in Mason County. Levels vary house-to-house — the only way to know your home’s level is to test it.

🏚

Older homes test high more often

Pre-1990 construction often used unsealed crawlspaces, gravel sub-slab fill, and lower-quality foundation seals. All three increase radon entry. Homes built into hillsides have additional radon entry paths through retaining walls.

🫁

Long-term exposure causes cancer

EPA estimates radon causes approximately 21,000 lung cancer deaths annually in the US — second only to smoking. Combined smoker + elevated radon exposure compounds the risk dramatically. Testing is cheap; treatment is expensive.

When You Should Test for Radon

EPA recommends every home be tested at least once. These are the situations where it’s genuinely time-sensitive.

🏡

Buying or selling a home

Increasingly common in inspection contingencies, especially for older homes, homes with crawlspaces, and homes in known radon zones. Standard 48-96 hour tests fit within typical inspection windows.

🆕

You’ve never tested

If you’ve never tested your home, EPA recommends doing so once. Long-term tests (90+ days) give the most accurate annual-average reading and are especially worthwhile for homes you plan to live in for years.

🛠

After foundation work or remodeling

Foundation crack repairs, basement finishing, slab modifications, or any work that disturbs the soil-foundation interface can change radon entry rates. Re-test after major work.

🔄

Post-mitigation verification

If a mitigation system was installed, EPA recommends re-testing every 2 years to confirm it’s still working. Fan failures and ventilation changes can silently raise levels back up over time.

🏘

Neighbor tested high

Radon levels can vary significantly house-to-house even on the same street, but a neighbor’s elevated read is a useful signal that your home is worth testing too.

Most-asked questions from Peninsula homeowners and real-estate buyers deciding whether to book.

Radon is a colorless, odorless radioactive gas from naturally occurring uranium in the soil. It seeps into homes through foundation cracks, crawlspace soil, and gaps around pipes. Long-term exposure is the #2 cause of lung cancer after smoking. The Peninsula has higher-than-average radon zones.

Short-term testing uses a continuous radon monitor or charcoal canister placed in the lowest livable level for 48-96 hours, closed-house conditions. Long-term testing (90+ days) uses alpha-track detectors for an annual-average reading. We pick the right method based on situation.

EPA recommends action above 4 pCi/L. WHO recommends action above 2.7 pCi/L. There is no truly “safe” level — risk scales with concentration and duration. Below 2 pCi/L is generally considered low risk. Our reports include measured level, EPA reference, and clear recommendation.
