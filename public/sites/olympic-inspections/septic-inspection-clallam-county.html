What’s Included in a Septic Inspection

A thorough evaluation of tank and drainfield, with a written report formatted for the county health district, lenders, realtors, and escrow. No pump-and-find tactics — we don’t profit from finding extra work.

📐

System location & mapping

Locate the tank and drainfield using as-built drawings on file with the county, plus on-site verification. We document the system layout for the report and for future inspections.

🪣

Tank assessment

Open accessible tank lids, measure sludge and scum levels, evaluate baffles and inlet/outlet condition, check structural integrity. Document everything with photos and measurements.

🌱

Drainfield evaluation

Visual evaluation of the drainfield surface for symptoms of failure: ponding, lush green vegetation, sewage odor, soggy ground. Document any signs of stress.

📄

Written report — county-ready

Photo-supported report formatted for the county health district’s reporting requirements (each county has its own format). Filed on your behalf when applicable. Copy to you, copy to the county, copy to your realtor or lender.

🔄

Pumper coordination if needed

If the inspection finds the tank needs pumping, we coordinate with vetted local licensed pumpers and re-inspect after pumping if required by your situation. We don’t pump ourselves — that’s deliberate.

📞

Scoping call before booking

We confirm your county, system type (gravity, pressure, advanced treatment), and reason for the inspection (bi-annual vs real estate vs concern) on the phone before booking. Written quote first — no surprise add-ons.

When You Need a Septic Inspection

Three situations cover almost all septic inspections in Washington State.

📅

Bi-annual WA state requirement

Most permitted residential systems require an inspection every 2 years, with some advanced treatment systems requiring annual inspection. The county health district enforces this and tracks compliance.

🏡

Real-estate transaction

Lenders require a septic inspection for any real-estate transaction on a property with a private septic system. Reports are formatted for buyer/seller use, escrow, and lenders. We work around tight closing timelines.

⚠️

Symptoms of system trouble

Slow drains throughout the home, sewage odor near the tank or drainfield, ponding water or unusually lush vegetation over the drainfield, or sewage backups. Don’t wait — small problems become expensive failures fast.
