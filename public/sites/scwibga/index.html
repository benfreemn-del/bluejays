Our Mission

We change the economic landscape for women — one business owner at a time.

Southern Crescent Women In Business is a conglomerate of women-owned businesses and women in business who have come together to grow capital, voice, and influence in metro Atlanta's Southern Crescent.

We advocate for women business owners at the local, state, and national level. We connect first-time founders to mentors who've been in their shoes. And we built the first Women's Business Center on the Southside because too many of our members were driving an hour into Atlanta to find resources that should have been here all along.

Women's Business Center

The first one on the Southside. Built for us.

For decades, women-owned businesses in the Southern Crescent had to drive an hour into Atlanta to find a Small Business Development Center, a funding workshop, or a mentor in the room. We changed that.

  • Free 1:1 funding consultations · find the right SBA loan, grant, or revenue-based investor for your business
  • Business plan workshops · monthly cohort, 4 sessions, walk out with a real plan a banker will read
  • Marketing & brand audits · members get one full audit per year, free
  • Quiet co-working · drop in any weekday between 9am and 4pm

Meet the Founder

Ariel Shaw, MBA

Building the room she wished existed.

Ariel founded SCWIBGA because every networking event she walked into felt like the same six women repeating themselves. She wanted a community where the woman selling lash extensions in McDonough and the woman running a $5M agency in Decatur could build something together.

"We're not trying to be every chamber of commerce. We're a specific home for women who are tired of being the only woman in the room — and ready to fill that room with each other."

— Ariel Shaw, MBA · Founder & CEO

Advocacy & Policy

We show up where the decisions get made.

When the Henry County Commission writes ordinances on home businesses, when the Georgia State Capitol moves on small- business tax policy, when Atlanta MSA reshapes contracting opportunities — SCWIBGA members are in the room.

We don't lobby in the traditional sense. We organize. We send turnout to public-comment hearings. We write op-eds. We pair members who've been through a regulatory fight with members about to start one.
